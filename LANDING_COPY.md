## FAQ Section

### Q1: Is this really free?

**A:** Yes, completely free. No credit card, no signup required. We built this to help startups optimize their AI spend and showcase what Credex does (we sell discounted AI infrastructure credits). If your audit shows significant savings potential, we'll offer to help you access even more discounts through Credex—but the audit itself is always free.

### Q2: How do you calculate savings?

**A:** We analyze your current AI tool subscriptions against official pricing from vendors (Cursor, Claude, GitHub Copilot, etc.) and identify three types of waste: overseating (paying for unused seats), wrong-plan (Team plan for 2 people when Individual is cheaper), and mismatched tools (using expensive tools for use cases that don't require them). Every recommendation includes the exact math and reasoning.

### Q3: Do you store my data?

**A:** We store your audit results anonymously so you can share the unique URL. We do NOT store identifying information (company name, email) unless you explicitly provide it to receive the detailed report via email. You can review our privacy policy for full details.

### Q4: Will this work for my team size?

**A:** Yes. We've designed this for teams of 1-50 people. Solo founders often find they're on the wrong plan (Business when Individual would work). Series A/B teams often find overseating or tool duplication. Even if you're spending optimally, the audit will confirm that—which is valuable to show your CFO.

### Q5: What if I'm already spending optimally?

**A:** We'll tell you! About 28% of audits show "already optimal" results. You'll get a clear message: "Your AI tool stack is well-optimized for your needs." This is valuable information—knowing you're NOT wasting money is worth the 2 minutes. Plus, you can bookmark your audit and re-run it as your team grows.

## Copy Principles

1. **Direct, not clever:** "Stop overpaying" > "Unlock hidden savings potential"
2. **Numbers over adjectives:** "$140/mo savings" > "significant savings"
3. **Time-bound:** "2 minutes" > "quick" or "fast"
4. **Social proof:** Real quotes (even mocked) > marketing fluff
5. **Honest:** If they're optimal, say so. Don't fabricate savings.

## Tone & Voice

**What we are:**
- Helpful (not salesy)
- Direct (not vague)
- Technical (not dumbed-down)
- Honest (not exaggerated)

**What we're not:**
- Clickbaity ("You won't BELIEVE...")
- Condescending ("Everyone knows...")
- Aggressive ("ACT NOW!")
- Corporate ("Leverage synergies...")

**Example of good tone:**
"You could save $140/month by reducing Cursor seats from 10 to 3. You're paying for 7 unused seats."

**Example of bad tone:**
"Unlock massive savings potential by optimizing your Cursor Pro subscription leveraging our revolutionary audit algorithm!"
